## Joints

The hand is a complex structure, involving many different joints. The most important to understand pulley injuries are (from the wrist to the tip of the finger):

- carpometacarpal joint (CMC)
- metacarpophalangeal joint (MCP)
- proximal interphalangeal joint (PIP)
- distal interphalangeal joint (DIP)

## Bones of the finger

The bones forming the digit are four:

- metacarpal bone
- first or proximal phalange
- second or middle phalange
- third or distal phalange

This scheme is repeated for 4 of the 5 fingers. The thumb has only two phalanges, one distal and one proximal.
